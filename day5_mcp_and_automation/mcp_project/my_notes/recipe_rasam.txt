Recipe — Amma's Tomato Rasam

Servings: 4 | Time: 25 min

Ingredients
-----------
- 3 ripe tomatoes (chopped)
- 1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind (or 1 tbsp paste)
- 1.5 tsp rasam powder
- 0.5 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp jaggery
- Salt to taste
- 2 cups water
- Fresh coriander (handful, chopped)

For tempering
- 2 tsp ghee
- 1 tsp mustard seeds
- 1 tsp cumin
- 2 dried red chillies
- 1 sprig curry leaves
- 4 garlic cloves (crushed)
- A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Steps
-----
1. Soak tamarind in 1 cup warm water for 10 min, extract pulp.
2. In a pot, add tomatoes, tamarind pulp, rasam powder, turmeric,
   jaggery, salt, and 2 cups water.
3. Simmer on medium for 10 min until tomatoes soften and the rasam
   turns frothy on top. Do NOT boil hard — it kills the aroma.
4. In a small pan, heat ghee. Add mustard seeds; when they splutter,
   add cumin, red chillies, curry leaves, garlic, and hing.
5. Pour the tempering over the rasam.
6. Garnish with coriander. Serve with steamed rice and a dollop of ghee.

Notes
-----
- The "froth" stage is the secret. Once it foams, switch off.
- For a cold day, add 0.5 tsp crushed black pepper at step 2.
- Pairs perfectly with potato curry and pappadam.
